Our Nootropic Ingredient Categories

🐟

Omega-3 PUFAs

Found in: Oily fish, flaxseed, walnuts, algae, soybeans

DHA and EPA are structural pillars of every neuron. DHA accounts for up to 22 % of the brain's total phospholipids. Clinical studies show DHA supplementation reduces cognitive decline in aging adults and cuts depressive symptoms in Parkinson's patients by 50 %.

🍇

Polyphenols & Anthocyanins

Found in: Blueberries, black rice, red grapes, green tea, turmeric, olive oil

The most extensively studied nootropic class. They cross the blood-brain barrier — scavenging free radicals, silencing NF-κB, and activating SIRT1. Curcumin reduces Parkinson's motor symptoms. EGCG delays amyloid-beta plaque formation. Resveratrol enhances hippocampal connectivity over 26 weeks.

🌾

Dietary Fibers & Prebiotics

Found in: Oats, chicory, garlic, onions, leeks, lentils, mushrooms

Fiber is the gut microbiome's fuel. Beta-glucan, inulin, FOS, GOS, arabinoxylan, and resistant starch feed beneficial bacteria that produce SCFAs. A 10-day fiber intervention in Parkinson's patients showed measurable improvements in gut microbiota and reduced neurodegeneration markers (NfL).

🥚

Essential Amino Acids

Found in: Eggs, meat, fish, legumes, dairy, soy

Tryptophan is the precursor to serotonin; low levels accelerate excitotoxicity. Arginine supports nitric oxide production critical for memory. Methionine governs DNA methylation. A 12-week supplement of 7 essential amino acids improved cognition, social interaction, and wellbeing in adults 55+.

🥕

Carotenoids & Terpenoids

Found in: Carrots, spinach, kale, tomatoes, citrus oils, herbs, spices

Lycopene activates the Wnt/β-catenin pathway — stimulating new neuron birth. Lutein regulates mitochondrial respiration. Lutein + zeaxanthin supplementation for 1 year improved attention and cognition in adults with mild dementia. They also reinforce the gut-brain axis from inside out.

WHAT IS IT?

Your Gut Is Your Second Brain

The Gut–Brain Axis (GBA) is a bidirectional communication network linking the central nervous system with the enteric nervous system of the gastrointestinal tract. This superhighway uses neural, hormonal, and immunological pathways to keep your body in balance.

Scientists now recognise the gut microbiome — trillions of bacteria, fungi, and viruses — as a key regulator of brain chemistry, mood, immunity, and cognition.
